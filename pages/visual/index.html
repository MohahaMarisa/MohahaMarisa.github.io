Winning Infographic

Summer 2016 ||| Skills: Graphic Design, Illustrator CC

The team I led, 'Infinity & Below' with the lovely and talented Tiffany Lai and Susie Lee, successfully researched and refined a visual storytelling of EGS potential for positive impact across spectrums. In the final rounds of the D.O.E's competition, we spearheaded a national social media campaign having researched and developed strategies for creating posting content.

The pictorial organization of the infographic allows for a better scrolling experience. As the information narrows down to be more specifically about geothermal energy, viewer is brought closer to the earth's core.

RESEARCH

The infographic challenge gave us the opportunity to tackle any topic regarding geothermal energy. My focus was on how we might be able to tell a story both visually and through content. I directed our team to first individually research topics that interested us. As the team lead I made sure to read up on everyone's topic lightly to better facilitate the group's discussions when we reconvened to consolidate a story line. After multiple discussions, we narrowed down on what and how the information would be experienced by our audience. We decided to target millenials that aren't as aware of geothermal or the general context of America's energy landscape.

Below are some of my sketches and notes from my research

ITERATION

Once the concept sketches, composition, and general color theme were determined, we worked in parallel to get the three distinct sections of the infographic geography pixel perfect.

POLISH

Putting all of our work together and editing from there. The final below utilizes a slight gradient on the objects to procide greater visual unity and coherency as one landscape. The long, strongly vertical format allows for a unique viewing experience when scrolling through it. The viewer is brought deeper into the Earth as the details get more and more detailed about Enhanced Geothermal Systems.

Each section of the landscape follows the visual and informational hierarchy of our argument. The sky gave a broad overview of the current context for why new alternative energies should be considered. Having establshed the need, the mountain range covers the economic benefit of possible enhanced geothermal systems relative to other sources before delving more deeply into what and how an EGS works.

Select promotion material

Part of the competition included a self-driven social media campaign. Our research and social media strategy proposal is HERE

Logo video above is an SVG animated in Framer.

We made a parody of the typical 'raising awareness' video about energy. Information is presented through humorous, pun-filled blurbs.

Promotional Poster

Fall 2016 ||| Skills: Graphic Design, Photoshop CC, Illustrator CC, InDesign CC

This poster is to promote a lecture series by modern pioneers in new media arts and computational design that are known for integrating code wih aesthetics. In my explorations, I take into consideration how the medium and the poster's process itself might implicitly communicate the idea of "Code + Form".

As with any poster, one must communicate an effective informational hierarchy through type, grid strucutre, composition, color and imagery while engaging the viewer's visual interest.

I began the process with typographical exercises to establishing informational hierarchies. How might simply tabbing, bolding, and spacing affect the viewer's understanding of the information?

Having played with color, I was excited to begin integrating images and trying different methods of generating visual assets with code. Iteration is essential to the process.

Time constraints meant I was unable to explore all of my breadth in depth, but I'm adequately satisfied with the latest iteration. The diagonal formatting and ambiguity of order for the title “code + form” effectively plays on the integrated computational art and design of the speakers presenting. It is compositionally dynamic, yet consistent between picture and type — balancing cohesion with unconventional grids. The color choices augment the informational hierarchy and provide suitable contrast and excitement. The image asset is an genertive art peice by Casey Reas.

Phipps Plant Conservatory

Fall 2016 ||| Skills: Photoshop CC, Painting

Phipps Conservatory, known for it's beautiful and eclectic collection of plants from all around the world, is a great place for color studies. I created a lookbook showcasing the colors of Phipps while maintaining their brand identity.

The final was a digitally painted translation of a color and photo collage of plants at Phipps conservatory. The iterations of the photo collage explore balancing color transitions and visual "noise" within the spreads. I ended up enjoying the complimentary color study of a cadmedium orange and a light phthalo green the most.

The breadth included everything from traditional media with acrylics and watercolor to extreme crops of photo collages and various photoshop manipulations.
